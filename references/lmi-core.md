# LMI Core

This skill uses a practical interpretation of LMI as a management discipline:

- clarify the role
- define meaningful and measurable goals
- connect goals to time investment
- identify high-return activities
- review results and adjust behavior

Use this framing when the user asks what good LMI practice looks like.

## Key Ideas

- A role is broader than a job description. It describes the value the person must create.
- Responsibilities explain what must be carried. Key results explain what must be achieved.
- High-return activities are the few actions or management moves that most improve results.
- Time investment is a management decision. What gets scheduled gets protected.
- Reviews are only useful if they change focus, mechanism, or behavior.

## Planning Chain

Use this chain as the default logic:

`Role -> Annual or half-year direction -> Monthly focus -> Weekly outcomes -> Daily execution -> Review and adjustment`

## Manager Lens

When the user is acting as a manager, always check:

- what only they can do
- what should be delegated
- what should become a management mechanism
- what should be tracked in a one-on-one or rhythm meeting
